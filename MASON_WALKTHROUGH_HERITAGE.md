## The honest diagnosis

- **The bottleneck is volume, not conversion.** Closing 3 of 3 is elite; the entire job is
  *more at-bats* — leads into a funnel that already converts.
- **A great business that's invisible.** The opportunity is the gap between the *quality*
  and the *visibility*.
- **What does NOT apply yet (and Mason says so):** Database Reactivation (#3) — repaint
  cycles are years out; no dormant goldmine in 15 one-year-old jobs. The 15 clients are
  gold for **referrals**, not repaint reactivation. *Telling the boss this is the
  production-honesty rule in action.*
- **Reviews can't be bootstrapped (key fact).** The 15 happy clients have *already* left
  their reviews — that's the 7–9 — and there's no backlog. At 3–4 leads/mo, new reviews
  only trickle. So the free "review flywheel" everyone preaches **won't start Heritage's
  engine**: a chicken-and-egg — low leads → few jobs → few new reviews → can't rank → low
  leads. **Something must inject leads before the flywheel can spin.**

## The real play order (revised for Jack's facts — reviews tapped, must inject leads)

1. **Get found — GBP optimization** — claimed ≠ optimized: weekly project-photo posts, full
   categories, service pages, Q&A, NAP + local SEO/**AEO**. Free, compounding visibility.
2. **Show the work** — big Main Line projects on social, organic *and* **targeted paid to
   affluent local homeowners**; the luxury visuals are the unfair advantage.
3. **Systematize referrals (#6)** — the 15 already vouched (they reviewed); now ask for
   **intros/referrals** — warm, free leads from the network Heritage already runs on.
4. **Targeted paid — LSA + Meta (#4), sooner than the generic playbook** — with no review
   fuel and no dormant list, paid is the realistic lead *injector* that breaks the chicken-
   and-egg.
5. **Reviews velocity (#1)** — still capture one from **every new job**; compounds as volume
   grows (just can't bootstrap from a tapped base).
6. **Speed-to-lead (#2)** — set up so nothing slips; low priority at 3–4 leads/mo, scales up.
