**Researched:** 2026-06-14 (live web). Two clusters matter: **trades-specific
players** (our real competitors) and the **horizontal AI engines** (better
mechanics, not built for the trades). Our edge is combining them — see
[STRATEGY.md](STRATEGY.md).

## What works best (cross-cutting patterns)

1. **Productized fixed tiers** beat custom quotes for SMB trades (Predictive Sales).
2. **"AI employee / marketing team"** framing reframes price as cheaper-than-a-hire.
3. **Exclusive territory** — premium + retention lock (Market Movers, Coherency, Minyona).
4. **Niche by trade** — generic agencies fail; trades want clarity, speed, proof.
5. **"System, not parts"** — disconnected tactics are the #1 reason contractor marketing fails.
6. **Outcome/ROI proof** over impressions (Minyona's pay-per-appointment).
7. **Intent-data targeting** — property/storm/income-zone signals.

## The gaps nobody fills

- **Real outbound for the trades** (trades players are inbound; outbound tools aren't trades-native or compliance-built).
- **The closed loop** — generate the lead *and* book it. JobMagnet + FirstBack.
- **Compliance-by-design** for cold outreach (TCPA/CAN-SPAM/DNC) — our moat.

## Deeper read — 2026-06-15 (why the leaders are better than us)

A second pass focused on *what gives each its edge* — the honest answer to "why
are they so much better." See economics in [MARKET_ECONOMICS.md](MARKET_ECONOMICS.md).

### GoHighLevel — the gravitational center
The all-in-one most agencies privately white-label. Their edge = **breadth +
white-label SaaS mode + the "AI Employee" suite** (Voice AI, Conversation AI,
Reviews AI, Content AI, Funnel AI) at ~$97–497/mo + $97/mo AI add-on.
**But its weaknesses are our opening:** steep 2–4 week learning curve, each tool
shallower than the dedicated point tool, recurring email-deliverability complaints
(Mailgun), 10–60s page loads, weak CRM, and — critically — **it's a generic
toolbox you assemble, not trades-native, and it does not book the job.** We don't
out-feature GHL; we out-*focus* it.

### FSM incumbents moving into marketing (ServiceTitan / Housecall Pro / Jobber)
Their edge = **they own the job + customer data**, so attribution and review-timing
are native. ServiceTitan's Marketing Pro is the strongest (micro-targeted
campaigns, call→booking attribution). Housecall Pro = basic email/postcard; Jobber
= almost none. **Threat:** they own operations data we'd want. **Opening:** they're
ops-first, marketing-bolt-on, enterprise-priced, and not demand-*creation* engines.
FirstBack/JobMagnet pairs as the demand+booking layer they lack.

### Reputation point tools (Podium / Birdeye / NiceJob)
Edge = **deep, proven review automation** (SMS invites, AI responses, 20+ site
aggregation). Pricey: Podium $399–599/mo, Birdeye $200–700/mo. NiceJob is the SMB
pick (Jobber/Housecall integrations, month-to-month). We don't beat them on depth —
we **bundle "good enough" reviews into the trades-native loop** so a contractor
doesn't buy a separate $400/mo tool.

### The structural thesis on our side: vertical SaaS wins
Vertical SaaS grew ~31% ARR vs ~28% horizontal in 2025; **~35% lower churn**
because narrow focus = deeper fit + painful switching costs. "Build for everyone,
build compromises that satisfy no one." This is the macro tailwind behind
niching hard on the trades vs GHL's everything-for-everyone.

### The frontier nobody owns yet: AEO/GEO
Homeowners increasingly ask ChatGPT / Google AI Overviews "best roofer near me"
*before* visiting any site. An AI marketing engine is uniquely positioned to win
"get found by AI" (answer-first content + schema + per-location pages). Few trades
agencies ship this — land-grab feature. See [MARKET_ECONOMICS.md](MARKET_ECONOMICS.md) §4.

### Net: where we can actually be better
1. **Trades-native focus** (vertical-SaaS retention) — not GHL's generic toolbox.
2. **The closed loop** generate→book→**cost-per-booked-job** — no point tool sees it.
3. **Compliance-built outbound** — horizontal cold tools aren't; agencies don't do
   outbound at all.
4. **Meet them in the truck** (photo-by-text), price as a fraction of ad budget.
5. **AEO land-grab** + automatable schema/GBP edges agencies skip.
