## Playbook #1 — The Review Velocity Engine  *(Phase 1, lowest risk, fastest proof)*

**Goal:** a *sustained drip* of fresh, real reviews — velocity, not a one-time blast —
because recency beats volume and it compounds into map-pack + LSA rank within 30–45 days.

**The play:**
1. **Trigger on job completion (warm).** Every finished job → request within ~1–2 hours,
   while the glow is fresh. This is the velocity engine's fuel.
2. **SMS first** (3x the response of email), one-tap direct Google review link, in the
   owner's first-name voice referencing the *specific* job.
3. **One polite reminder** ~3 days later if no review — multi-touch lifts response, but
   never nag past that (we're not spammers).
4. **Ask everyone — never "gate."** A "how'd we do?" check-in is fine, but routing only
   happy customers to Google and burying unhappy ones **violates Google policy** — JobMagnet
   won't do it. Unhappy → a fast *service-recovery* alert to the owner (fix it), with the
   customer still free to review. Honesty *is* production.
5. **Respond to 100% of reviews within 72h** (Google reads responses; it lifts rank).
   AI-drafted, owner-approved (Pro) or auto (Premium).
6. **Measure:** reviews/30 days (velocity), star avg, sentiment — tied through to map-pack
   rank, LSA cost-per-lead, calls, and ultimately **cost-per-booked-job (M10)**.

**Compliance gates:** SMS only to consented current/past customers, opt-out honored, quiet
hours; **no review gating, no incentivized or fake reviews, ever** (FTC + Google).

**Done when:** Heritage sustains a measurable monthly review velocity and every review gets
a response — and we can show the rank/lead lift it produced.

## Playbook #2 — The Speed-to-Lead Engine  *(the #1 lever, co-first with reviews)*

**Goal:** every inbound lead gets a real response in *seconds*, because the first
responder wins **78%** of multi-quote shoppers and a 5-min reply converts up to **100x**
better than 30 min. A contractor on a ladder physically can't do this — JobMagnet always can.
This is the single biggest gap between "has leads" and "books jobs."

**The math (why it's free money):** missed-call text-back recovers **30–60%** of unanswered
calls into booked jobs; SMS converts ~**45%** with engaged contacts. Worked example (HVAC,
from the data): 80 calls/mo, 50 unanswered → 40% reply (20) → 30% book (6 jobs) ×
$400 = **$2,400/mo recovered** on calls that were already coming in. A painter's job value
is far higher — the recovered number is bigger.

**The play:**
1. **Capture every inbound, one intake** — missed call, web form, GBP message, FB/IG DM,
   LSA lead. Nothing falls through.
2. **Missed-call text-back in 5–15 seconds:** owner's voice, e.g. *"Hey, it's Heritage
   House Painting — sorry we missed you, we're on a job. What do you need? I'll get you a
   call right back."*
3. **Web form / message → instant acknowledgment + one qualifying question** within seconds.
4. **Multi-touch until they answer:** ~2 min → 1 hr → 24 hr, then stop / hand to owner.
5. **AI qualify + book:** JobMagnet asks the 2–3 that matter (service, address in territory,
   timeline), then **books to the calendar or hands the owner a hot, qualified lead with
   context.** (FirstBack runs the booking/voice; JobMagnet feeds it.)
6. **Hot-lead alert, in voice:** *"Hot one — Sarah in Lititz, kitchen cabinets, wants a
   quote this week. I've got her on text. Want me to set the estimate, or you calling?"*
7. **Speed SLA:** target <60s first touch, measured every lead.

**Measure:** % of leads actually contacted, median time-to-first-touch, lead→booked rate,
missed calls recovered → **cost-per-booked-job (M10)**.

**Compliance:** this is *responding to an inbound* (the customer contacted first) — the
safest posture, not cold outreach. Still honor opt-out; quiet hours apply to follow-ups,
not the immediate reply.

## Playbook #3 — The Database Reactivation Engine  *(found money, zero ad spend)*

**Goal:** turn the dormant past-customer list into booked jobs on predictable **repaint
cycles** — **5–6x cheaper** than new leads, and a list almost no contractor ever touches
(dental practices sit on $500K–$1.5M in dormant value; a painter's list is the same idea).

**The play:**
1. **Ingest the customer list** (CSV/import): last-job date, service type, address.
2. **Cycle model per service** (when a job comes "due"):
   - **Interior:** 3–7 yrs; high-traffic (kitchen/bath/hallways) **2–4 yrs**; low-use 10+.
   - **Exterior:** 5–10 yrs; **PA climate (snow/rain) pushes it to the short end** —
     wood siding 3–7, vinyl ~10, brick 15–20.
   - **Cabinets / specialty:** tracked separately.
3. **Trigger on the window, in voice:** *"Hey Sarah — we did your interior back in spring
   2021. With the hallway taking a beating from the kids, might be about time. Want me to
   pencil in a free walkthrough?"*
4. **Cross-sell on the cycle (value-based targeting):** did interior 3 yrs ago → now pitch
   the **high-margin** job (cabinets, exterior) the contractor actually wants.
5. **Multi-touch:** 4–5 spaced touches lift reactivation **81%** (SMS → email → SMS over a
   few weeks); stop on response or opt-out — never nag.
6. **Seasonal timing:** fire interior/cabinet reactivation into the **slow winter** (fills
   the slow season — straight out of the Mandate); fire exterior reactivation late-winter
   to book the spring/summer season *before* competitors wake up.
7. **Piggyback referral + review:** a warm reactivation touch is also the moment to ask a
   happy past customer for a referral or a fresh review (feeds Playbook #1 velocity).

**Measure:** dormant-list size, reactivation rate, recovered revenue, and its
cost-per-booked-job vs. new-lead cost (it'll be a fraction).

**Compliance:** past customers are a warm, established relationship (low risk) — but the
consent ledger, opt-out, and quiet hours still apply. JobMagnet **never** cold-texts
non-customers; that stays gated (M5).

## Playbook #4 — GBP + Review-Fed LSA Paid Play  *(when paid is warranted)*

**Goal:** when the contractor is ready to spend, buy the *cheapest qualified* leads —
LSA at ~**$60**/lead and **20–25%** conversion (vs 6–8% PPC). And the kicker: **Playbook #1
makes this cheap**, because reviews are the #1 controllable LSA-ranking factor.

**The play:**
1. **Foundation before spend:** don't run paid until GBP is optimized and review velocity
   (#1) is live — otherwise you pay full price for poor rank.
2. **Set up LSA + the "Google Verified" badge** (license/insurance/background check);
   tighten **categories + service-area zips** so you're not billed for junk leads.
3. **Pace within the Mandate ceiling;** start small, scale only what books; **dispute**
   out-of-area/spam leads to recover spend.
4. **Then Search Ads** (high-intent keywords, AI copy), **then Meta** retargeting with
   before/afters for the visual trades — in that order.
5. Feed all spend + results into **cost-per-booked-job (M10)**; kill what doesn't book.

**Measure:** CPL, lead→booked, cost-per-booked-job by channel, badge status.
**Compliance:** low — honest claims only (real licensing/insurance for the badge).

## Playbook #5 — Offer + Risk-Reversal Builder

**Goal:** most contractors have **no real offer and no guarantee** — and offer strength is
*inversely* correlated with CAC (**80%** of winning med-spa campaigns open with a
low-barrier offer). Give every campaign an honest, compelling hook + risk reversal.

**The play:**
1. **Low-barrier entry offer:** free color consult, free walkthrough/estimate, "$X off if
   booked this month," or a **winter interior special** that fills the slow season.
2. **Risk-reversal / guarantee:** satisfaction guarantee, workmanship warranty,
   "we're not done 'til you're happy" — removes the fear that kills the booking.
3. **High perceived value, always honest** (soul rule): bundle smartly (interior + cabinet
   package); never promise what can't be delivered.
4. **Honest urgency:** real seasonal windows and limited slots tied to actual capacity
   (from the Mandate) — never fake countdowns.
5. **Aim the offer at the goal job** (value-based): push cabinets / whole-house, not
   one-rooms. Deploy across social, GBP, reactivation, and LSA landing pages.

**Measure:** offer take-rate, CAC by offer, booked jobs.
**Compliance:** honest advertising, no bait-and-switch (FTC).

## Playbook #6 — Maintenance-Plan + Referral Engine

**Goal:** recurring revenue + a referral flywheel off the existing base — retention is
**5–7x cheaper** than acquisition, and a customer on a plan stops calling competitors.

**The play:**
1. **Maintenance/touch-up plan:** annual touch-up visit, exterior wash, warranty check,
   priority scheduling → steady scheduled work + lock-in.
2. **Referral program:** unique code/link, **tiered rewards** (bigger for higher-tier
   jobs), asked at peak happiness — job completion or right after a 5-star review.
3. **Personal touch:** handwritten-style thank-you, loyalty discount on next service.
4. **Communication discipline:** 24-hr-before confirmations, a dedicated point of contact,
   **post-service SMS survey** (3x the response of email).
5. **JobMagnet orchestrates:** spots who just had a great experience → fires the referral ask;
   tracks plan renewals so nothing lapses.

**Measure:** plan enrollment, referral rate, repeat rate, LTV.
**Compliance:** outreach consent; referral incentives fine (disclose); **never** incentivize
fake/positive-only reviews.

## Playbook #7 — AEO / Get-Cited-by-AI  *(the land-grab almost no PA contractor is in)*

**Goal:** be **the** answer when someone asks ChatGPT / Google AI / Perplexity "best painter
in [town]." AI Overviews now hit **~55%** of searches; ChatGPT has **883M** users; **83%**
of AI citations come from pages updated within 12 months.

**The play:**
1. **Answer-first local content:** pages that directly answer real questions ("how often to
   repaint a house in PA," "cost to paint kitchen cabinets in Lancaster") — doubles as the
   reactivation/education content.
2. **Entity clarity + structured data:** LocalBusiness + Service + FAQ schema, consistent
   NAP, crisp what/where/why-relevant.
3. **Per-location / per-service pages** generated from the Business Brain.
4. **Freshness:** JobMagnet refreshes pages on a cadence (<12 mo) — that's where 83% of
   citations come from.
5. Lean on reviews + GBP as the trust signals AI reads.

**Measure:** AI citations, AI-referral traffic, "near me"/AI visibility, leads contributed.
**Compliance:** low — accuracy only (don't publish claims AI will repeat).

## #8 — Value-based targeting
Not a standalone play — it's the thread through #2–#7: always steer toward the **highest-LTV
job the contractor wants** (cabinets, whole-house), the way dental pushes implants. Encoded
in the Mandate ("going after / easing off") and obeyed by every playbook above.
